Editorial Concept & Visual Narrative

For my Master's project at LABASAD, I designed CRUDA, an editorial magazine that explores the synergy between humans and nature. I curated real photographic content by @miriaamani, focusing on the Hmong ethnic minority in Vietnam, and structured it to tell a cohesive visual story.

Through my work on typography, layout systems, and prepress processes, I translated documentary photography into an organic editorial language, ensuring a publication that is both visually compelling and production-ready for print.

Visual Design

The magazine's aesthetic balances clean layout, rhythm, and structured editorial spaces. Utilizing a cohesive color palette and precise typographic scale grids, the design elevates the photographic material, creating a high-end visual pacing that guides readers effortlessly through different sections.

Narrative & Storytelling

CRUDA combines visual storytelling with carefully styled text blocks, including an intimate interview with the photographer @miriaamani. This dual approach offers deeper insights into the photographer's perspective and the complex cultural context surrounding the Hmong community.

Content Curation

The curated publication selectively integrates high-contrast photography highlighting daily life, customs, and deep-seated spiritual rituals. The careful curation of these image selections ensures a narrative sequence that honors both documentary journalism and contemporary editorial design.
